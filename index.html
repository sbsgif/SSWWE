The First International Conference on Sustainable Synergies: Waste, Water, and Energy for a Greener Future (SSWWE 2025) builds on the success of SRWWE, the national edition, now expanding to a global stage. This conference brings together experts to explore waste assessment and valorization, water treatment, renewable energy and energy efficiency, and environment and material degradation, fostering collaboration for a more sustainable future. üìö Key Topics üìö

This conference will encompass eight key topics, providing a comprehensive exploration of sustainability challenges and solutions:

Sustainable Waste Valorization & Circular Economy

Innovations in recycling, upcycling, and waste-to-energy solutions, integrating AI for smarter waste management.

Water Management, Treatment & Resource Resilience

Advanced purification methods, AI-driven smart water systems, and resilience against emerging contaminants.

Renewable Energy & Energy Efficiency Innovations

Breakthroughs in solar, wind, bioenergy, and AI-powered energy optimization for sustainable development.

Sustainable Materials & Environmental Resilience

Development of eco-friendly, durable materials with life-cycle assessments to minimize environmental impact.

Artificial Intelligence in Sustainable Development

AI applications in climate action, smart cities, predictive analytics, and intelligent waste management.

Global Policies, Partnerships & Community Engagement

Strengthening international collaborations, policy frameworks, and local sustainability initiatives.

Education and Capacity Building for Sustainability

Integrating sustainability into education, fostering research, and equipping communities with green skills.

Recycling of Polymeric & Polymer Composite Materials

Advanced recycling technologies for polymers and composites, including AI-driven sorting and chemical depolymerization.

Prof. Abdel-Hamid I. Mourad

United Arab Emirates University (UAEU)

A distinguished professor in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at the (UAEU). His research has resulted in more than 200 referred and peer-reviewed international journal articles and conference papers. According to ResearchGate, he has 328 publications, 6,403 citations, and an h-index of 37. The AD Scientific Index 2025 reports an h-index of 47 with 8,261 citations. He has been recognized among the World's Top 2% Scientists by Stanford University and received the UAE University Award for Excellence in Scholarship for the academic year 2020-2021. Prof. Mourad serves as Editor-in-Chief of the Emirates Journal for Engineering Research (EJER) and is a member of the Mohammed bin Rashid Academy of Scientists (MBRAS).

Prof. Abdeltif Amrane

Institute of Chemical Sciences of Rennes, France

A full Professor at the University of Rennes 1. (Google Scholar: h-index: 41; citations: 6289 // Web of Science: h-index: 32; citations: 4106). Since approximately 13 years his research is entirely devoted to the development of combined processes for the removal of organic pollutants in effluent wastewaters and gaseous emissions within the CIP team. He has managed 9 PhD theses as thesis director and 3 are in progress; he has also co-managed 6 PhD theses and 2 co-management theses are in progress. He has been involved in several projects as a manager or a participant, as well in international collaborations (Algeria, Belgium, Hungary, Iran, Lebanon, Mexico, Morocco, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Spain, Tunisia). He has published about 330 international papers including 17 papers in press. He has also published 12 chapter books and has about 130 international and 20 national oral communications.

Prof. Dr. Yogendra Kumar Mishra

University of Southern Denmark (SDU)

A Professor at (SDU) and head of the Smart Materials Group, specializing in functional nanomaterials such as porous 3D networks, flexible ceramics, and nanofibers, with applications in sensors, biomaterials, and energy storage. His research has garnered over 22,200 citations, with an h-index of 78 and an i10-index of 294 (Google Scholar). He has supervised numerous Ph.D. students and postdoctoral researchers and has been honored as a Fellow of the Royal Society of Chemistry (FRSC), UK, along with several other awards. Prof. Mishra serves on the editorial boards of leading journals, including Nano Energy and Materials Today Chemistry.
